Dos During Pregnancy

Below are the Dos during pregnancy.

The Dos during pregnancy that are good for you and your baby are:

Take Folic acid and vitamin A

Folic Acid reduces the baby’s risk of neural defects such as spina bifida. Take 400mg of folic acid as a supplement as soon as possible until the end of the first trimester. If you have diabetes or epilepsy, a higher dose maybe required and this may be available on prescription.

Take Vitamin D supplement

It is recommended during pregnancy and breast feeding. It helps a baby develop health bones, teeth and muscles.

Eat a balanced diet.

It is important to eat well, have a balanced diet which includes a variety of foods like fruit, vegetable, meat, cheese, potatoes, beans and pulses. Ask your midwife for support in case you’re struggling with your diet.

Exercise Regularly

Staying active will help you sleep better, reduce anxiety and help you stay healthy during pregnancy. Listen to your body and slow down if you begin to feel uncomfortable. Managing Moodswings.

Being pregnant is an emotional experience and it is normal to have mood swings. However, seek for help incase these feelings become unmanageable or last longer than a couple of weeks.

Symptoms to look out for during pregnancy.

There are symptoms that should always be checked with the midwife or doctor as they could be a sign that the baby is unwell.

  • Painful urination
  • Bleeding from the vagina
  • Sudden and continuous abdominal pain or cramps
  • Severe headache
  • Baby’s movements slowing down
  • Blurred vision, spots in front of eyes
